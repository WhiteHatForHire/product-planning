NEXT 30 DAYS — FULL STACK REBUILD SPRINT

Week 1: Cursor Mastery + Next.js Muscle

Goal:

You can scaffold, debug, refactor, and deploy a modern app without friction.

Build:

A simple SaaS skeleton:

Auth

Dashboard

CRUD table

Deploy to Vercel

No overengineering.

No database complexity.

Just movement.

Week 2: Backend/API Refresh

Build:

Simple FastAPI service:

3 endpoints

Basic validation

Connect to Postgres

Deploy to Render

Now connect frontend → backend.

You’ve restored full loop.

Week 3: Multi-Tenant Logic

Add:

User roles

Organization accounts

Data isolation per org

This is architect-level muscle.

Week 4: Real Tool

Build:

A tool that you would actually charge $2–5k for.

Example:

Internal KPI dashboard generator.

Client automation system.

Lead scoring mini-tool.

Deploy.

Document.

Polish.

That’s portfolio-grade.

Now about the emotional high.

You’re feeling like:

“I could build studios.”

“I could be one of those NYC guys.”

“I could 10x this.”

You probably could.

But the trap here is breadth.

The brain wants to open 6 tracks at once:

Agency 2.0

VST

Games

Python tools

NYC network strategy

Business restructure

That leads to fragmentation.

We do one track.

Full stack rebuild.

Everything else is dessert.

Now here’s the grounded truth:

Engineers are not obsolete.

Bad engineers are.

The ones who survive are:

Architects.

Product-aware.

AI-augmented.

Systems-first.

You’re aiming to be that.

Good.

But that requires boring reps.

Boring reps are the price of leverage.

Let’s zoom out philosophically for 30 seconds.

You took seven years.

Lived.

Built.

Loved.

Traveled.

Ran business.

Failed.

Succeeded.

Now you return to tech not as a 22-year-old chasing syntax.

But as a 30+ systems thinker with lived context.

That’s the right arc.

Not late.

Not early.

Right arc.

Now I’m going to challenge you gently.

You said:

I’ve augmented my brain to think with AI.

Good.

But the real test is this:

Can you still think without it?

Because if AI disappears tomorrow,

do you still understand the system?

That’s the bar.

Use AI as acceleration.

Not replacement.

Now let’s lock something in.

Next 30 days:

You will not:

Restructure the agency.

Announce a pivot.

Build a VST.

Build a game.

Start 6 repos.

You will:

Build one serious full-stack system.

Deploy it.

Polish it.

Write a technical breakdown of it.

That’s it.

Answer me one precise thing:

If you had to build a tool in 30 days that would genuinely make a NYC founder say “oh shit,”

what category would it be?

Internal ops?

Automation?

Analytics?

AI workflow tool?

Creative tool?

Pick one.

We anchor it.
