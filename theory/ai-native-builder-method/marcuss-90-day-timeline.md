Marcus’s 90-Day Timeline

April 22, 2026 to July 20, 2026

Core thesis

This 90-day block is not about mastering everything. It is about becoming:

90 days sober or meaningfully on-track

dangerous with Replit and modern AI-native building

stable enough to ship consistently

credible enough to help at least one real business owner

alive enough to keep music and writing in the system without letting them hijack the season

The order is:

Sobriety and stability

AI-native building fluency

Music as emotional fuel and creative regulation

Writing as integration, not pressure

Relationships and recreation in right proportion

90-day outcome targets

By the end of this 90-day block, the win looks like this:

You have maintained a serious sobriety streak and built a real recovery rhythm

You have shipped 3 to 5 real Replit applications

You are comfortable with:

Replit workspace

databases

auth

secrets

deployments

API integrations

basic debugging and iteration

You have rebuilt or meaningfully upgraded one real business-facing asset

example: Eagle Rocket site, internal tool, calculator, dashboard, intake tool

You have at least one real-world consulting proof point

even a free or low-cost pilot counts

You have kept writing alive through field notes, chapter seeds, and selected sessions

You have kept music alive as a living source of expression, not just a someday identity

The governing principle

This season is about praxis over fantasy.

Not:

learning every tool

making endless artifacts

branding too early

building a giant theory tower

But:

sober repetition

real builds

useful systems

clean follow-through

Rules for the AI-native path

Rule 1

Replit is the main tool for the first month.
 Do not dilute the season by constantly switching platforms.

Rule 2

Only learn tools when they solve current problems.
 Do not study LangGraph because it sounds advanced.
 Study it when an actual workflow needs it.

Rule 3

A shipped ugly tool beats a beautiful unfinished system.

Rule 4

Artifact creation supports building. It does not replace building.

Rule 5

Your future agents should reduce busywork, not replace your own understanding.
 First learn the craft, then delegate the drudgery.

Rules for writing

Allowed

chapter seeds

short reflective bursts

Not required in this 90-day block

finishing a book

publishing a book

Rules for music

Music is not procrastination if:

it regulates you

it reconnects you to aliveness

it remains bounded

it does not cannibalize sleep or building

Music is part of the system. Not a distraction from it.

confusing stimulation with progress

turning AI ambition into the new addiction

using travel or movement to avoid structure

The clearest and most energizing path right now is not “prompt engineer.”

Why the AI-native path should lead this season

Building apps is currently more digestible than writing books.

It provides:

a path toward helping real people soon

The likely better order is:

builder first, writer supported later by the builder

In other words, if you become more dangerous in applied AI, you will later be able to build systems and agents that help with the books, organization, summarization, synthesis, and busywork.
