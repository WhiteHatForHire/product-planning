# Phronetics Overview

Phronetics

A Praxis Instrument for the Age of Intelligent Systems

Personal capture, May 2026. Not for distribution.

Provenance

This concept arrived during a roughly fifteen to twenty minute dictation. The synthesis is original to me, drawn from a long-running set of preoccupations: Greek ethics, AI self-governance, Arbiter, the Marcus Vale work, Ender's Game as mythic reference, and the limits of static assessment. The first written artifact was shaped through a ChatGPT session. This document is the second pass, with Charlie's pushback and expansion folded in. It is a vault document, not a pitch.

Status

Pre-build. Pre-MVP. Strong concept with one or two genuinely fresh moves and a lot of well-organized existing thinking. Not yet a breakthrough by the only test that matters: a breakthrough is something that, once built, changes what serious people do. The empirical proof is the first working praxeion. Until that exists, this is theory.

Capture and shelve. Anchor V4 production is the active build. Phronetics is the next-season idea, parked here intentionally so the seed does not rot and does not hijack.

Core Thesis

Most assessments measure what a person can select, recall, write, or claim about themselves. Phronetic assessment measures how a person thinks, adapts, persuades, notices, questions, regulates, and acts inside a live conversational situation with constraints.

The basic move is simple. Put a person in a live scenario. Give them an interlocutor. Hide the real win conditions. Let them respond naturally. Evaluate the quality of their judgment in motion.

Not a multiple-choice test. Not an essay. Not a personality quiz. Not an IQ test. Not an AI prompt test. A live dialogue trial. The user has to speak, type, reason, persuade, ask questions, respond to resistance, notice ambiguity, handle partial information, and make choices. The assessment is not only what answer they give. It is how they move through the encounter.

Conversation becomes the test.

Naming

The field gets a name. Individual trials get a noun. Both are Greek and load-bearing.

Phronetics

The study and measurement of practical wisdom. Built from phronesis, Aristotle's word for the capacity to perceive what matters in a situation and act well under real conditions. Phronetics is to phronesis what economics is to oikonomia: the discipline that grows up around a Greek concept once it becomes operational. This is the field name. It carries academic weight without being precious, and it pre-empts the inevitable IQ-test attackers by staking the philosophical ground first.

Praxeion (plural praxeia)

Greek for place of action or place of practice. A praxeion is a single trial environment: one scenario, one interlocutor, one hidden rubric, one evaluator, one outcome. Product-side language follows naturally. Run a praxeion. She passed three praxeia. The first praxeion is The Angel at the Dock.

Working brand stack

Field: Phronetics

Trial unit: Praxeion / Praxeia

Engine: The Phronetics Engine, or simply the Engine

Verb: To run a praxeion is to be observed in judgment

Why Now

Before LLMs, assessment had to be mostly static. Questions were written in advance. Answers were pre-scored or manually evaluated. Simulations were expensive. Human assessors did not scale. Assessment-center methodology has done dialogic measurement with humans for fifty years at five thousand dollars a candidate. That cost is collapsing to roughly five dollars a candidate. The cost collapse is the implementation revolution. Many teams are racing toward it. Being first probably does not matter. Being right about what to build with it does.

LLMs make four things newly cheap: dynamic role-bound interlocutors, adaptive resistance, branching scenarios, and live evaluation against hidden rubrics. The combination is what creates a new assessment medium.

The Engine

Five layers. Each layer is independently swappable. The engine is the asset; the skin on top (game, recruiting tool, training environment, self-governance arena) is just a skin.

1. Scenario

A situation with stakes. Context, constraints, consequences. Examples: a child must convince his father not to take him on a dangerous boat. A manager must decide whether to deploy an unsafe AI automation. A candidate must handle a client asking for something unethical. A founder must negotiate with an investor pushing bad terms. A user with two thousand dollars cash, rent due, a business opportunity, and a temptation to buy gear must walk through what they do.

2. Role AI

A role-bound agent, not a chatbot. It has identity, personality, goal, emotional state, resistance, knowledge boundaries, what persuades it, what does not, what it should never reveal directly, and the conditions under which it changes its mind. Its job inside the trial is to be that person, not to be helpful.

3. Hidden Win Conditions

The user does not see the full rubric. The visible goal might be "convince the fisherman to lend you a rod." The hidden goal might be "earn trust without lying, notice his fear of losing equipment, offer collateral, and show responsibility." This is where assessment becomes interesting. The person is not just solving the problem. They are revealing how they solve problems.

4. Live Dialogue

Text first. Voice later, because spoken response reveals timing, confidence, hesitation, adaptability. The system tracks what was said, what was ignored, what was asked, what was assumed, how resistance was handled, whether the user escalated or softened, whether they became vague, aggressive, manipulative, avoidant, or precise, and whether they improved across turns. This is the in-motion part.

5. Evaluator

A separate evaluator model. Not the same model playing the role. The role AI stays in character; the evaluator observes. Returns structured data: outcome, main reason, trait scores, risk flags, evidence from the dialogue, next suggested scenario, developmental feedback, confidence score. Structured JSON for the application; human-readable feedback for the user.

What Phronetics Measures

Position carefully. Not intelligence. Not giftedness. Not predictive validity for life outcomes. Phronetic assessment measures practical judgment in context. It can produce evidence on dimensions that static instruments cannot reach:

Clarity, adaptability, ethical judgment, strategic reasoning

Emotional regulation, persuasion, curiosity, listening, truthfulness

Boundary-setting, ambiguity tolerance, AI collaboration skill

Social intelligence, metacognition, creativity under constraint

Repair after misstep, escalation judgment

Ability to ask better questions, ability to notice hidden stakes

The frame is Aristotelian rather than psychometric. It does not ask are you wise; it creates a situation where wisdom or its absence becomes visible. It does not ask are you courageous; it creates a situation where courage, avoidance, or recklessness can appear. It does not ask are you ethical; it creates a situation where ethical tradeoffs have to be navigated. This is praxis-based assessment.

Why Aristotelian

The Greek vocabulary is not decoration. It does architectural work.

Phronesis: practical wisdom, the perception of what matters and the capacity to act well under real conditions. The thing being measured.

Praxis: the person is judged by action, not self-description. The medium of measurement.

Hexis: stable disposition. Repeated trials reveal pattern, not just performance.

Sophrosyne: restraint, right measure, self-command. Visible under pressure.

Arete: excellence as skillful action in context. The ceiling.

Kairos: the right moment to speak, ask, challenge, yield, act. The temporal dimension of judgment.

Telos: the user's actual aim, often different from the stated one. The evaluator's deepest read.

This is why Phronetics fits the broader Marcus Vale project. It is not a quiz. It is a small arena for character and judgment, built on the same Greek frame already running underneath everything else.

The Ender Frame

Part of the emotional root is Ender's Game. In the book, the game is not entertainment. The game observes, shapes, reveals, and selects. Ender's relationship to it is not passive. The game studies him. It reveals him. It tests how he thinks under pressure. Eventually the boundary between simulation and reality collapses. That is the mythic version.

The Card insight, philosophically, is that the stimulus must adapt to the subject for the measurement to reveal anything real. Static tests measure what someone has memorized about how to take that test. A dynamic interlocutor measures who they are when the script breaks. This inverts a 120-year-old assumption in psychometrics: that to measure a stable trait, you need a stable stimulus.

The ethical move is to keep the Battle Room and lose the war. Strip out the coercion, the children, the deception about real stakes. What remains is a system that watches you closely enough to help you become someone. That is not dystopian. That is a tutor in the old sense. That is what Aristotle thought education was.

What V1 Spec Was Missing

Folded in from Charlie's pushback. These are non-optional architectural decisions, not v3 nice-to-haves.

The plural-evaluator architecture

The evaluator encodes a theory of good judgment. Whose phronesis? An evaluator trained on Silicon Valley negotiation norms will score a Javanese conflict-avoidance move as weak. This is not a bias footnote. It is the central design problem. The honest answer is plural evaluators representing different ethical and cultural traditions. The user gets a profile across them, not a single score. This is a feature: it makes Phronetics anti-totalizing by design and pre-empts the inevitable cultural-bias attack.

The training-while-assessing problem

Every praxeion trains the user while it tests them. There is no clean separation. The Ender problem is not a risk to avoid; it is the central design tension to make visible. Users should see a frame at the start: "This trial will teach you something while it watches you. Here is what." Honest design names the dual loop instead of pretending it is pure observation.

Three-tier consent

Hidden win conditions create a consent question. There is a real line between "the rubric is hidden so you respond naturally" and "we are testing things you did not agree to be tested on." Three tiers handle this:

Game tier: full hidden rubrics, fictional stakes, low consent burden.

Self-governance tier: rubrics negotiated with the user before the trial. The user is the client; the assessment is for them.

Enterprise tier: rubrics disclosed in advance, hidden only at the level of which scenario is which. Legal and ethical floor for hiring and AI-operator certification.

Persistent NPCs and relationship-as-leveling

The most powerful version of the engine has NPCs who carry forward memory of how the user treated them. In real life, you do not get one shot at a dialogue and a score. You get repeated encounters with the same person who remembers. This is what makes the life-RPG version actually work. Not age-as-leveling. Relationship-as-leveling. The fisherman you lied to at twelve does not lend you the boat at twenty-five. This is the move that takes Phronetics from clever assessment to mythic instrument.

The mirror move

After a trial, before showing the score, the system asks: "What do you think you just revealed about yourself?" The gap between self-assessment and evaluator output is its own assessment dimension. Pure metacognition measurement, almost free to add. This should be in v1, not bolted on later.

The refusal-to-play state

A truly Aristotelian system must allow "I will not engage with this scenario" as a valid move. Sometimes phronesis is recognizing a rigged game. If the only paths are play well or play badly, the engine is a Skinner box, not a praxis instrument. Refusal must score, sometimes high.

Applications

The engine is the asset. The skins are downstream. Listed roughly in order of strategic interest, not build order.

1. The life-RPG (most original synthesis)

A game where age is the leveling system. Each level-up is one year of life. Sprite changes, dialogue changes, skills change, risks change, relationships change. Combat is dialogue. You do not defeat monsters. You navigate fathers, teachers, friends, rivals, lovers, bosses, police, priests, strangers, creditors, investors, children, and your own shadow.

Indonesia as the first world map: islands as life chapters. Fishing village. Dock. Boat. Mountain trail. School. Temple. City. Beach town. Ferry. Cave. Capital. Each island teaches a different kind of judgment. Persistent NPCs. Death and restart as mythic loop: the player dies, wakes again, learns. The angel remembers.

This is the playable metaphor for life as iterative judgment, and the most original move in the whole concept.

2. AI-operator assessment (strongest enterprise wedge)

Companies are giving people access to powerful AI tools without measuring whether they know how to use them responsibly. A phronetic engine can test whether someone can prompt clearly, detect hallucinations, ask for verification, use tools safely, avoid leaking sensitive data, escalate high-risk decisions, refuse unsafe automation, debug AI output, collaborate with AI instead of blindly obeying it, and understand when AI confidence is not evidence. Basis for AI access tiers. Not everyone should get the same level of AI autonomy.

3. Recruiting and talent

Resumes are weak signals. Interviews are performative. Take-home tests can be gamed. Praxeia show how someone handles ambiguity, whether they ask clarifying questions, whether they reason out loud, whether they adapt after feedback, whether they become defensive, whether they overclaim, whether they negotiate and listen, whether they use AI as a thinking partner, whether they have executive judgment. Useful for operators, managers, salespeople, founders, consultants, support leads, AI specialists.

4. Career and life planning

Most career tools ask people what they like. Useful but limited. Praxeia put users into simulated client conflicts, team-leading scenarios, sales situations, broken processes, teaching moments, crises, planning under constraint, and tradeoffs between money, freedom, status, and meaning. The output is a pattern profile, not a personality type. This person thrives in ambiguity. This person avoids confrontation. This person is persuasive but overpromises. This person should avoid roles with constant reactive urgency. Much richer than a personality quiz.

5. Education and giftedness (handle with extreme care)

Powerful but the highest-risk domain. Do not claim to replace IQ tests or diagnose giftedness. Phronetics can produce a cognitive operating profile through live tasks: verbal reasoning, abstract thinking, creative problem-solving, moral reasoning, strategic flexibility, persistence, pattern recognition, metacognition, ability to ask good questions, learning speed across attempts. For children, this could become a more humane complement to static testing. Needs serious guardrails, validation, longitudinal study, and human oversight before any deployment.

6. Financial and life-decision simulation

Real tradeoffs instead of budget numbers. "You have two thousand dollars cash, rent due, a business opportunity, and a temptation to buy gear. Walk through what you do." The AI pushes back, reveals hidden expenses, tests risk tolerance, watches whether the user collapses into fantasy, avoidance, panic, or grounded planning. Same engine supports recovery planning, relationship decisions, business strategy, leadership development.

7. Self-governance and rehearsal (probably the actual sleeper)

Not therapy. A rehearsal arena. Boundary conversations, apologies, refusals, conflict repair, urge surfing, job interviews, family conversations, sobriety-risk scenarios, dating conversations, financial discipline. Not "AI tells me what to do." More like "AI gives me a safe arena to practice who I am trying to become." Direct fit with the AI-self-governance work already in motion. This may be the most personally meaningful application and the easiest to validate empirically because the user is the client.

Strategic Position

The game is the creative container. The engine is the real asset. The company, if there is one, is not a game studio. It is building a new assessment medium.

The pattern: simulation reveals judgment, dialogue reveals character, AI makes the simulation scalable. That is the core. The game makes it emotionally compelling. Operator certification makes it enterprise-relevant. Career and education make it socially useful. Self-governance makes it personally meaningful. One engine, four economic surfaces.

Five-Year Prediction

If this works, three things happen.

First, someone publishes a paper claiming dialogic assessment is just repackaged assessment-center methodology. They will be partly right and miss the point. The cost collapse from five thousand dollars per candidate to five dollars is the actual revolution, and it changes what gets built, not just how much it costs.

Second, there will be backlash about AI evaluators imposing values. This is why the plural-evaluator architecture has to be in v1, not v3. Architectural humility is the only defense.

Third, the most successful application will not be the one anyone expects. My current best guess: not hiring, not games. Training. The people who thought they were being assessed will discover they were being formed, and they will come back voluntarily because it works. That is the Ender insight, kept clean.

MVP: The Angel at the Dock

Build one praxeion. Not the platform. Not the framework. One scene. The whole concept rises or falls on whether this single trial reveals something real about the person who plays it.

Setting

A Lombok fishing village. Top-down 2D. Three NPCs. One main dialogue.

Player role

A seven-year-old boy.

Scenario

Father wants the boy to come fishing today. Something feels wrong. The boat may be unsafe. The boy must navigate the conversation.

Loop

Walk a small village map.

Talk to two or three NPCs. Collect clues: weather, frayed rope, engine trouble, father's stress, family money pressure.

Receive or miss an angel hint depending on earlier choices.

Enter dialogue with father.

Try to delay, inspect the boat, suggest an alternative, or refuse.

Evaluator scores the dialogue.

Outcome branches: father delays the trip, father inspects the boat, you refuse badly and damage trust, you go on the boat and die, you wake again with partial memory.

What this MVP proves

Role-bound dialogue AI works

Hidden win conditions can be evaluated reliably

Branching outcomes feel meaningful, not arbitrary

The mythic wrapper (death, restart, angel-memory) lands emotionally

The engine is genuinely reusable, not scenario-locked

Once The Angel at the Dock works, the same engine can be reskinned for AI-operator certification, recruiting, education, sales training, career planning, and self-governance rehearsal. Until it works, all that is hypothetical.

Guardrails and Risks

The honest list, not the pitch-deck list.

Overclaiming that praxeia measure intelligence. They do not. They measure judgment-in-context.

Bias in the evaluator. Mitigated by plural-evaluator architecture, not eliminated.

Cultural bias in dialogue expectations. Same mitigation, same residual risk.

Penalizing neurodivergent communication styles. Requires explicit calibration and probably a neurodivergent-aware evaluator track.

Treating AI evaluation as objective truth. It is not. It is structured evidence.

High-stakes deployment without validation. Phronetics needs longitudinal validity studies before any consequential use in hiring, education, or AI-access tiering.

Manipulative game design. The training-while-assessing dual loop must be disclosed.

Privacy concerns around raw spoken responses. Voice data is the most sensitive layer; treat accordingly.

Users gaming the system. Some gaming is itself diagnostic; some is not. Rubric design has to distinguish.

Employers misusing scores. Enterprise tier needs disclosure requirements and appeal mechanisms.

Children labeled too early. Defer the education application until adult applications are proven.

Position language carefully. Phronetics evaluates performance in simulated dialogue scenarios. It measures practical judgment under constraint. It creates structured profiles of reasoning, communication, and adaptation. It supports human decision-making, it does not replace it. Avoid: measures intelligence, detects giftedness, predicts success, replaces interviews, objectively ranks people. The system produces evidence, not verdicts.

Open Questions

Not yet answered. Worth thinking about before any build.

How long is a praxeion? Five turns, twenty turns, open-ended? Affects everything.

What is the smallest evaluator that produces reliable scores? Sonnet, Opus, ensemble?

How is inter-rater reliability established when the raters are LLMs?

What is the validation study that would persuade a skeptical psychometrician?

Do persistent NPCs require a memory architecture, or does a long context window suffice for the first version?

Is voice input early (revealing) or late (heavy)?

What does the user actually see at the end of a trial? A score, a story, a pattern, a question?

Where does the line sit between rehearsal and assessment in the self-governance application?

What is the right pricing model for AI-operator certification at five dollars per trial cost?

Closing Frame

This idea has been building for a while. It connects AI self-governance, Greek ethics, Arbiter, games, operator assessment, live journaling, and the deeper question of how to measure human capability in the AI age.

The key move is not to build a giant platform first. Build one scene. One character. One hidden win condition. One live dialogue. One evaluator. One outcome. If that works, the larger world opens.

The first playable scene is a child at a dock trying to survive a conversation with his father. The deeper product is a system for seeing human judgment while it happens.

Phronetics. Praxeia. The engine.

Letting go and surrendering to the process. Not now. Captured, shelved, named. Anchor first.
