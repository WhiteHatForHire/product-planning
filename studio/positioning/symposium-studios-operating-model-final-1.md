Status:  Internal strategy document. Use this to guide offer design, first conversations, pricing, lead generation, capacity management, and delivery boundaries. Do not publish verbatim. External materials should be shorter, buyer-facing, and outcome-led.

Operating answer:  Yes, Symposium Studios can start consulting now. The right entry is bounded: paid discovery, AI workflow audits, prototype sprints, internal tool builds, and fractional technical direction. The wrong entry is overselling enterprise agentic transformation before the delivery system, entity rails, and case-study base are mature.

1.  Core Thesis and Positioning Rule

Core thesis:  Symposium Studios should enter the market as a boutique AI implementation and software systems studio for founder-led and operator-led teams. The commercial wedge is practical: turn messy workflows, product ideas, and AI opportunities into working software with senior technical judgment, clear scope, review gates, and human approval.

Positioning rule:  Externally, lead with outcomes, not method language. Do not lead general buyers with 'AI-native,' 'Director Model,' 'Council of Models,' or deep agentic vocabulary. Lead with workflow pain, internal tools, prototypes, LLM-integrated applications, measurable operational improvement, and senior judgment. Use the deeper method language only in sophisticated rooms or after trust is established.

North-star sentence:  Symposium Studios helps founders and operators turn messy workflows, product ideas, and AI opportunities into working software.

2.  Pipeline Thesis

Symposium should not depend on cold inbound in the first 90 days. The initial pipeline should come from warm trust, physical presence, existing agency credibility, targeted conversations, and visible proof. The public brand creates legitimacy, but the first deals will come from direct conversations.

6.  Market Pricing Signals as of May 2026

The market supports premium pricing, but buyers pay premiums for specificity, trust, speed, and risk reduction. The lesson is not 'charge enterprise prices immediately.' The lesson is to avoid low-end freelancer anchoring and sell bounded outcomes.

Major consulting and cloud firms are moving toward fixed-price, outcome-based, and AI-assisted delivery models rather than pure hours.

Symposium should quote fixed outcomes first and use internal hourly math only for margin and scope control.

12.  Discovery Call Qualification

A good first call prevents bad work. The goal is not to prove AI is magical. The goal is to find a valuable, bounded workflow and decide whether Symposium is the right operator.

What workflow is currently costing you time, money, missed leads, or management attention?

Who touches this workflow today and where does the data live?

What would be visibly better 30 days from now?

What systems must this integrate with: CRM, email, calendar, forms, Slack, website, database, payment, spreadsheets?

What data is sensitive, regulated, client-owned, or not allowed to touch third-party AI?

Do you need autonomous action, AI-drafted recommendations, or just better visibility?

Who approves changes and who will use the system daily?

What budget range is realistic if the opportunity is real?

What would make this project not worth doing?

15.  Availability and Time-Zone Constraints

Symposium can serve NYC clients while Marcus is traveling, but paid retainers require clear availability windows. Bali is UTC+8 — 11 to 12 hours ahead of NYC EDT. The natural overlap window falls in the early morning Bali / evening NYC range (approximately 7–9am Bali / 7–9pm EDT). Define your available call window explicitly before committing to any retainer, and hold it.

Do not sell a high-touch fractional CTO retainer unless the calendar can support it.

Avoid daily real-time dependency unless priced as high-touch retainer work.

Prefer async-first delivery with scheduled review blocks.

Use written specs, build logs, Loom or video walkthroughs, decision memos, and acceptance checklists to reduce meeting load.

During travel-heavy weeks, sell audits, reviews, and scoped deliverables before high-touch implementation engagements.

18.  Year 1 Target Shape

The first 90 days establish proof. The operating picture to build toward: one reliable repeatable offer (likely AI Operations Audit into Prototype Sprint), one or two active build engagements at a time, one advisory retainer from a client with ongoing need, and a subcontractor or DevOps partner available for execution leverage on larger builds.
