Owner: Marcus Vale (founder, Symposium Studios).
Parent entity: Bisel Legacy Global LLC.

## 1. Purpose

symposiumstudios.ai is the business surface for Symposium Studios: the praxis site where the studio's services, proof, working method, and contact path live. It is *praxis* to marcus-vale.com's *theoria*. The job is to convert qualified interest into business conversations by demonstrating credibility, lane, and senior judgment in under 60 seconds.

The studio's public lane is LLM-integrated apps, interactive LLM systems, founder prototypes, internal tools, product MVPs, and serious custom software built with senior technical direction. Symposium is not a generic web shop and not an AI hype agency. It selects for projects where LLMs are part of the product architecture, not pasted onto it as marketing.

## 2. Primary Audiences

- Founders building LLM-integrated apps or AI-enabled product concepts
- Operators with messy internal workflows that need custom software
- Founders making interactive LLM systems: games, simulations, assessments, adaptive experiences
- Investors with portfolio companies needing fCTO or technical product judgment
- Teams considering a build but unsure how to scope it
- Tech Week and event acquaintances who want to evaluate Marcus quickly
- Potential collaborators (other operators, designers, builders)

## 3. What a Visitor Must Understand in 60 Seconds

1. Symposium Studios builds LLM-integrated apps, interactive systems, prototypes, internal tools, and product MVPs.
2. The studio operates with senior technical direction and disciplined delivery, not agency drag.
3. Anchor is live, deployed proof of building serious software in a sensitive domain under tight review constraints.
4. Symposium is led by Marcus Vale, who has eight years running a software services agency.
5. There is a clear path to start a conversation.

## 4. Brand Role and Relationship to Marcus Vale

Symposium Studios is the shop. marcus-vale.com is the mind. Symposium carries the work; Marcus Vale carries the worldview.

Marcus Vale is the public home for the deeper method vocabulary: Director Model, Council of Models, Pattern Intimacy, AI Self-Governance, the changing unit of work. Symposium Studios names the work lane in concrete terms (LLM-integrated apps, interactive LLM systems, prototypes) without using the method-IP language. The same operator runs both. The split exists because the audiences and the jobs are different.

When a sophisticated prospect on a sales call asks "how do you move this fast?" the agentic build method becomes an in-conversation disclosure, not homepage marketing.

The cross-link is quiet:

`Read Marcus Vale on systems, judgment, and the changing unit of work.`

No essay teasers on the Symposium homepage. Visitors who want philosophy follow the link. Visitors here want execution.

## 12. Visual Direction

- Color palette: same family as marcus-vale.com (ivory, near-black, stone, muted gold) but cooler and more architectural. Slightly higher contrast, more generous negative space.
- Typography: clean modern type pairing. Heading sans (Söhne, Inter Display, or Söhne-adjacent), body sans (Inter or IBM Plex Sans). Optional small caps or letter-spaced labels for section headers, evoking inscription without temple cosplay.
- One classical motif used sparingly: a meander, a column fragment, an inscription line. Used once or twice, never as a recurring pattern.
- Diagrammatic linework over decoration where any visual is needed.
- No stock imagery. No AI-generated art. No people photographs.
- Mobile: single-column, generous whitespace, large body type.

The site should feel like an architecture firm's project page or a fashion-house workshop, not an AI startup landing page.

## Symposium Studios Specifics

### Public-Facing Lane vs Internal Method

Symposium runs internally on AI-native infrastructure: a director-led build pattern, model councils for high-stakes decisions, agentic implementation, structured review gates, build logs, and the AUTONOMY_LAYER protocol. None of this internal vocabulary appears on the homepage in V0.

The public lane is a different layer. Symposium publicly sells LLM-integrated apps, interactive LLM systems, founder prototypes, internal tools, product MVPs, and serious custom software. AI and LLM appear in concrete product terms (what gets built), not in method-IP terms (how the studio builds).

The method-IP language lives on marcus-vale.com (essays, concepts, theory). Symposium's homepage shows the outputs and the product lane. When a sophisticated prospect asks on a call "how do you move this fast?" the agentic build method becomes a conversational disclosure. The buyer should discover a sharper operator, not land on an AI hype page.

### How AI and LLM Appear on the Public Site

In concrete product terms. The lane signal lives in:

- The hero ("LLM-integrated applications")
- The subhead ("AI-enabled concepts")
- The What We Build categories (LLM-integrated apps and sites; Interactive LLM systems)
- The How We Work method paragraph (modern AI and LLM tools as part of the studio's real capability)
- The Offers section (LLM App Build; Interactive Systems and Games; Technical Direction and fCTO Support)

These are not hype mentions. They name actual product capability and engagement scope. The verifier rule is: AI and LLM may appear where they describe real capability, scope, or method. They may not appear as empty marketing filler.

### Anchor as Proof of Serious Software in Sensitive Domains

Frame Anchor without leading with AI as a marketing term. The proof is the discipline, not the buzzword:

`Anchor is a live product in a sensitive domain, built under tight review constraints with safety routing, scenario testing, and production deployment discipline. It demonstrates the studio's standard for shipping serious software where quality and judgment matter.`

The sensitive domain (recovery) is named once as context for why review constraints mattered. The recovery-specific audience continues to be served by sobrietyanchor.com. Symposium's audience is operators and founders evaluating the studio's ability to ship serious software, including LLM-integrated software.

### Protected Names

- Phronetics
- Arbiter
- Propylaea
- DreamMirror

If any are surfaced later, ship only with explicit approved public-safe copy.

### Phronetics-Adjacent Category Without Naming

If language is needed to cover reflection/assessment work without naming Phronetics, use the existing "Interactive LLM systems" category. The "games, simulations, assessments, and adaptive experiences" framing covers the territory generically.

### Phrasing Operator History Safely

Use:

`Symposium is led by Marcus Vale, who has eight years of experience running a software services agency, with direct experience in client delivery, agency operations, and full-cycle software engagements.`

Avoid:

- "Symposium grew out of Eagle Rocket"
- "Symposium's roots are in Eagle Rocket"
- "Eagle Rocket's team is now Symposium"
- "Founded by the team behind Eagle Rocket"

Eagle Rocket is operator track record. Symposium is a separate studio.

### Handling "We" Language Honestly

"We" refers to the studio operating system: the operator plus internal tools, review processes, and the method advantage that lives on Marcus Vale. The site does not pretend to be a human team. If a section feels like it is faking headcount, rewrite to "the studio" or first-person singular.

For V0, use "we" naturally without disclaimers. The plain language and outcome focus make the studio's structure feel obvious without needing a transparency note.

### Differentiated Offers in Plain Buyer Language

Seven engagement types, in the locked copy doc. Named for the outcome the buyer wants, with LLM and AI capability surfaced where the engagement actually involves it:

1. Prototype Sprint
2. LLM App Build
3. Interactive Systems and Games
4. Internal Tool Build
5. Product MVP Build
6. Product Rescue and Rebuild
7. Technical Direction and fCTO Support

These are differentiated because they describe outcomes, posture, and lane. No "audit + recommendations" framing.

### Communicating Standards and Approach

Five principles, plainly stated, no paragraphs:

- Clear scope. Working software. No agency drag.
- Senior direction on every engagement.
- Review, scenario testing, and production discipline are part of the work, not extras.
- Client ownership of code, data, and operational keys.
- The studio owns the quality of what gets deployed.

The list IS the section.

### Linking Quietly to Marcus Vale

One line, footer or about:

`Read Marcus Vale on systems, judgment, and the changing unit of work.`

No essay teasers. No "From the founder's writing" homepage block. The link is enough. Sophisticated AI-curious readers follow it and find the full method vocabulary there. Outcome-focused buyers stay on Symposium and convert on the work.
