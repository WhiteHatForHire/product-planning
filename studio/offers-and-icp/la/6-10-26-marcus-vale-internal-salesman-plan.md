Marcus Vale Internal Salesman Plan

Fractional Agency Business Development / Technical Closer Strategy

1. Core thesis

My strongest monetizable lane is not generic software building, generic consulting, or normal employee sales.

My highest-leverage lane is:

Fractional business development partner / technical closer for founder-led agencies, AI shops, dev studios, RevOps firms, healthcare tech vendors, and digital transformation agencies.

The reason this works: I can sit between founder, buyer, and technical team. I have been a founder, CTO, agency operator, and closer. Most salespeople cannot credibly sell complex software, AI, healthcare, or technical agency work without overpromising. Most technical founders can explain the work but do not consistently create pipeline, expand accounts, or close with speed.

My edge is the combination:

$3M+ personally closed

Best year around $850k closed

Roughly $400k personal pay in peak year

Agency founder/operator background

Startup founder / BD background

Former CTO / fractional CTO credibility

Full-stack, app, AI-native, systems, and healthcare-tech literacy

Ability to sell complex services without sounding like a generic sales bro

Ability to understand whether delivery is real or vaporware

The market is changing. AI is making basic web/app building less differentiated. “I can build websites” is no longer scarce. The more durable and scarce skill is:

live-room trust, buyer psychology, deal architecture, technical translation, account expansion, and closing.

The plan is to use technical literacy as sales leverage, not to retreat into commodity building.

5. Offer model

Pure commission is dangerous unless attribution is extremely clean. The preferred model is:

90-day pilot: retainer + commission

Baseline structure

$2,500–$5,000/month retainer

10–15% commission on closed-won revenue I source and/or close

5–10% commission on account expansion I materially influence

6–12 month commission tail on introduced accounts

Clear CRM attribution

No unpaid delivery management

No employee-style 40-hour expectations

Commission paid on collected revenue

Deal registration for major prospects

Higher-end structure

For strong-fit agencies with proven delivery and $100k+ deal sizes:

$5,000–$8,000/month retainer

10–15% commission on closed revenue

3-month minimum

Option to expand into fractional VP Sales / Head of Growth

Finder-only structure

Only use for clean intros:

5–10% finder’s fee

Written deal registration

Commission tail

No ongoing sales work unless separately paid

Fractional VP Sales structure

Use only if they want strategy/systems, not just closing:

$8,000–$15,000/month

3–5% override on new revenue or growth

Defined scope: sales system, pipeline, messaging, coaching, proposal process

Not responsible for carrying the entire quota alone unless compensated separately

6. Contract rules / non-negotiables

The biggest danger is not failure to sell. It is being exploited or having commissions blurred.

Insist on:

Written agreement before meaningful selling

Clear definition of “sourced,” “influenced,” “closed,” and “expanded”

CRM as source of truth

Deal registration for named accounts

Commission tail for 6–12 months

Commission based on collected revenue

Payment schedule defined

No commission loss because agency mismanages margin

No unpaid proposal architecture beyond agreed scope

No responsibility for delivery unless separately paid

Termination clause that preserves commission on active pipeline

Right to refuse selling work that seems technically weak or ethically questionable

Key phrase:

“I’m happy to be accountable for pipeline and sales activity, but I need attribution and compensation rules clean before I put my name and relationships into the market.”

14. Internal operating rules

Sales identity rule

I am not begging agencies for work. I am evaluating whether their delivery deserves my sales reputation.

Technical credibility rule

I do not need to be the builder. I need to be technical enough to sell honestly, qualify intelligently, and protect the buyer from nonsense.

Commission rule

No attribution, no serious selling.

Partner rule

Bad delivery damages my name. Vet delivery before selling.

Focus rule

Sales is the main money lane. Building products is optional and opportunistic, not the default avoidance cave.

City rule

NYC is a sprint. LA is a base candidate. Indonesia is recovery/relationship/low-burn. Do not ask one city to do every job.

Body rule

Post-surgery recovery and nervous-system regulation are part of the business plan. No heroic overexpansion.
