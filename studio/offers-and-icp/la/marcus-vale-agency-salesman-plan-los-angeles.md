Marcus Vale Agency Salesman Plan
in Los Angeles

A practical plan for using an LA base to meet agency owners, get inside existing client trust, and generate expansion revenue.

Working document - June 2026

Core thesis

Do not build the whole client funnel from scratch. Get inside agencies that already have clients, trust, delivery, and account history. Become the operator who finds hidden expansion revenue and closes it.

1. Executive summary

This is the most translatable income lane: fractional/internal agency sales and account expansion. The move is not to wait for Forrest or any single operator. The move is to take the role he revealed and package it for LA agency owners who already have clients but are under-monetizing the relationship.

Positioning: Agency Account Expansion Operator / Fractional Upsell Lead.

Core promise: You already have clients. I help you find and close hidden expansion revenue inside those accounts.

Proof base: Roughly $3M sold through Eagle Rocket, agency delivery experience, and a credible technical/AI/software layer through Symposium.

Strategic base: A one-year LA lease gives routine, gym/beach regulation, and proximity to small agency owners, creative operators, founders, and production/tech rooms.

Qualification rule: No access, no magic. If the owner will not give access to account context, account admins, sales calls, or client conversations, the opportunity stays warm but inactive.

4. Why Los Angeles is the right base

LA is not just lifestyle. It is proximity infrastructure. The city has a dense mix of creative, media, advertising, production, startup, entertainment, creator, wellness, and tech-adjacent companies. That creates many small and mid-sized agencies where the founder has relationships but inconsistent internal sales follow-through.

Body regulation: A Westside/Ocean Park/Santa Monica/Venice-adjacent base supports gym, beach walks, cooking, sleep, and sober routine.

Founder density: Agency owners, production people, creators, marketing consultants, and startup operators are easier to meet casually in LA than through cold internet funnels.

In-person advantage: The pitch is much stronger over lunch or coffee: 'Tell me about your client base; I will show you where the expansion revenue is.'

Content advantage: Marcus Vale can continue public-facing video/comedy/AI self-governance work while the income lane runs through relationship sales.

6. The agency expansion sales process

Qualify the owner. Are they actually interested in expansion? Do they have existing trust and room to grow? Will they give access?

Map the account base. List active clients, past clients, retainers, project history, current contacts, pain points, budget signals, and relationship strength.

Find expansion angles. Look for stale websites, broken workflows, manual ops, poor reporting, weak landing pages, missing AI/automation, unused CRM, content bottlenecks, or adjacent service needs.

Create a simple offer ladder. Small audit -> implementation sprint -> retainer/maintenance -> larger transformation project.

Coordinate with account admins. Account people know the client context; Marcus owns the commercial opportunity and next-step push.

Join or lead client calls. Use consultative diagnosis first, then package the next scope.

Close and hand off. Make sure delivery team can fulfill. Do not sell chaos.

Track revenue and proof. Record pipeline, won revenue, case studies, and testimonials.

12. Scripts

12.1 Event opener

What kind of clients does your agency mostly serve? And who owns expansion after the first project is done - founder, account manager, or nobody officially?

12.2 Founder diagnosis question

If I looked across your current and past clients, would we find obvious expansion opportunities that nobody has had time to chase?

12.3 Access ask

For me to be useful, I would need account context: active clients, past projects, account notes, current contacts, and the ability to join or run the right client conversations. If you are comfortable with that, I can map the opportunities quickly. If not, I can only give outside advice.

12.4 Pilot close

Let us run a 30-day expansion sprint. I will review the client base, identify the top opportunities, coordinate with your account team, and help move the best conversations toward proposals. If we do not find real pipeline, we stop. If we do, we formalize the role.

12.5 Forrest-style reset message

I am still interested in helping with sales/account expansion, but to be useful I need actual access to sales calls, account-admin context, and client/account notes. Otherwise I am mostly guessing from the outside. When you are ready to plug me in concretely, send the invites/docs and I will prep properly.

15. Decision rule

LA lease is justified if it becomes an operating base for body regulation, creator output, and agency-owner proximity - not just a vibe-based city bet.

If the LA base produces meetings, lunches, rooms, calls, pilots, and creative output, it is working.

If it becomes expensive isolation, scrolling, and vague networking, correct quickly.

The offer works only through access, follow-through, and rooms. Do not let vague interest become your operating system.
