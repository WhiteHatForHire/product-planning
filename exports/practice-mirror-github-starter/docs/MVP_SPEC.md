# Practice Mirror - MVP Spec

## Problem

People often know a hard conversation is coming but do not practice it. They rehearse vaguely in their head, ask for scattered advice, or avoid the conversation until the stakes rise.

Practice Mirror gives the user a private place to run the conversation before it matters.

## Target user

Initial user: Marcus.

Likely later users:

- founders preparing calls
- people in recovery making sponsor or repair calls
- job seekers
- freelancers handling client friction
- people dating or rebuilding social confidence
- managers handling performance conversations

## Modes

### Hard Conversation

For boundary-setting, repair, confrontation, or emotionally loaded truth-telling.

### Interview

For job interviews, story practice, and follow-up questions.

### Sales or Client Call

For discovery, objection handling, proposal framing, and negotiation.

### Apology

For accountability, repair, and concise ownership.

### Asking for Help

For sponsor calls, mentor asks, friend support, and vulnerable requests.

### Social Invitation

For dates, dinners, invitations, and reconnecting.

## Session setup

Required fields:

- situation
- counterpart role
- desired outcome
- user's fear
- tone target
- hard constraints

Optional fields:

- relationship context
- known facts
- what not to say
- preferred difficulty
- practice length

## Live session

The live screen includes:

- scenario goal
- counterpart card
- emotional temperature
- transcript
- text input
- pause button
- coach hint button
- end session button

The counterpart should:

- stay in role
- respond naturally
- apply pressure consistent with difficulty
- reveal objections gradually
- avoid solving the user's problem for them

## Pause coaching

When paused, the coach can give:

- one tactical observation
- one suggested next line
- one risk warning

The coach should not produce a long essay during live practice.

## After-action review

The review includes:

- outcome
- what worked
- what weakened the user's position
- missed opportunity
- strongest line
- line to revise
- emotional read
- risk flags
- drill to repeat
- final script

## MVP exclusions

- Voice input/output.
- Real-time emotion detection.
- Calendar/contact integrations.
- Long-term memory.
- Multi-user sharing.
- Therapist mode.
- Automated message sending.

## Seed scenarios

1. Ask a collaborator to clarify ownership and next steps.
2. Tell a friend you need more direct communication.
3. Handle a client pushing scope without budget.
4. Practice a recovery call when tempted to isolate.
5. Ask someone on a low-pressure date.
6. Interview for an AI product engineering role.

## Success metrics

For the first private MVP:

- 10 complete sessions.
- 5 saved final scripts.
- User repeats at least 2 scenarios.
- Review output is specific enough that the user changes their wording.
