Provider Pitch

What Yuni reads before her first provider conversations.

Internal use  ·  English only  ·  Yuni adapts to each conversation

Read this first.

This isn't a script to recite. It's the mental model behind every provider conversation. Adapt the words; keep the substance.

The single most important framing:

"This is a system that sends better-fit foreign clients to providers who are good but underdistributed."

Not: "join our marketplace."

Not: "we're a startup, big things coming."

Not: "we'll change wellness in Bali."

The providers we want already have work. They don't need our pitch. They need us to make their existing work easier and slightly more lucrative without complicating their lives.

Who to target — and who not to

Target: good but underdistributed practitioners.

Therapists with strong skills but weak English / no Instagram / no SEO

Healers whose bookings come through word-of-mouth chains 5 people deep

Premium specialists fully booked 3 days a week, slow the other 4

Foreigner-friendly providers tired of sketchy WhatsApp inquiries

Quality villa-capable providers without their own logistics setup

Don't target:

Providers who are fully booked 7 days a week with their own client list — they don't need us, and pitching them wastes their time and ours

Spa employees — they don't control their own bookings

Anyone whose vibe feels off in the first five minutes — trust your gut, no exceptions on this

The core pitch — what to actually say

After greetings, after small talk, after the local context that you'll handle better than I ever could — the substance is this:

"There's a new platform for matching foreign clients to good local practitioners. It's not like the apps you know. The customer tells the system how they're feeling — sore, jet-lagged, anxious, post-yoga — and the system matches them to the right practitioner based on what kind of work they actually need. Not based on price or who's closest.

"What it means for you: you only get clients who fit how you work. Foreign tourists who'd otherwise end up at a hotel spa or a random Klook booking. The system explains why each client is right for you before they even contact you.

"How it works: I personally interview every practitioner before they're listed. Marcus built the technology side. Customers scan a QR code at their villa or guesthouse, describe how they're feeling, and the system suggests the best matches. I confirm the booking with you by WhatsApp before anything is final. The customer pays you directly in cash or QRIS — we don't take any money from you. We earn a small commission only on completed bookings.

"You stay independent. No exclusivity. Keep all your existing clients and word-of-mouth network. We just send you better-fit foreign clients on top of what you already have, especially during your slower days."

The objections — and the answers

"I already have enough clients."

Answer: "That's why I'm asking you specifically. We don't need providers who are desperate for work. We need providers whose work is good. The question isn't whether you need more clients — it's whether your Tuesday and Wednesday afternoons are as full as your weekends. That's where we help most."

"Why should I trust this when I don't know it?"

Answer: "You don't need to. Try it for two weeks. Take one or two of our matched clients. See if they're a better fit than your usual walk-ins. If yes, we keep going. If no, we stop and you've lost nothing. We never charge you anything. You only get paid for sessions you actually do."

"What about my regular customers?"

Answer: "They're yours. Always. We don't want exclusivity. Anyone you already work with stays with you, and the relationships you build through us — those become yours too. The only thing we ask is that we connected you, you don't actively poach from us. That's it."

"Foreign tourists are difficult / sketchy / cheap."

Answer: "That's exactly why this exists. The reason tourists are difficult is that they don't know what they need. The reason some are sketchy is that nothing screens them out. Our system asks about how they're feeling, not what's the cheapest option — so the customer who reaches you is already in the right mindset. And we screen for sexual-services language and intoxication before anyone gets to you."

"Can you accept foreign cards? My current clients pay cash and it's a hassle."

Answer: "Right now, it's still cash or QRIS — same as you're used to. In a few months we'll be able to accept foreign credit cards directly, take care of the conversion, and pay you in IDR within a day or two. That part is coming. For now, nothing changes for you on the payment side."

"What about safety / weird customers / late-night villa visits?"

Answer: "You can refuse any booking, no penalty, no explanation needed. Late-night requests have to be approved separately. If a customer is intoxicated or makes a sexual request, you leave with full payment and the customer is permanently banned. We default to believing the provider on safety claims. The system protects you, not just the customer."

For premium healers and specialists

Different conversation, different pace. Premium practitioners — the ones who charge Rp 1.5M–4M per session — don't need a pitch. They need a relationship.

Specifically for the people you already know — and the ones I'm aware of:

Amaji (master practitioner)

[FILL IN: 2-3 sentences. What she does, why her bookings are referral-only, what makes her a fit for our premium customer.]

Alexa / Alexi (Russian Karsai practitioner)

[FILL IN: 2-3 sentences. The Karsai work specifically, who her customer base is, why she'd or wouldn't be interested.]

[Russian healer — the mushroom one]

[FILL IN: name, what she does, why she's worth listing.]

[Other premium practitioners worth naming]

[FILL IN as you identify them.]

These are the practitioners worth approaching with patience. Don't pitch them in the first conversation. Just visit, share the idea, ask if they'd be open to talking again. The premium tier compounds slowly and we have to earn the right to send their clients to them.

What you actually need from them

If they say yes (or seem interested):

Schedule a 30-45 minute interview. Not a chat. A real conversation about their work, their style, who they work best with, where their boundaries are.

Get a recent professional photo. Or take one yourself if they don't have one.

Get one reference — someone who can vouch for them. You'll call this person.

Get their service menu: what they offer, how long, what price, where they'll travel.

Get their availability windows: which days, which hours, how much advance notice.

Get them to sign the simple agreement: no sexual services, no medical claims, respect boundaries, report incidents. One page.

Record a 60-second voice memo right after the meeting. The system will turn it into a structured profile.

Their first 3 bookings are monitored. After 3 clean sessions with positive feedback, they move to Tier 1 and get full matching weight.

Two things to remember

1. Their time is the constraint, not their willingness. A good provider with a full Friday and an empty Tuesday afternoon will say yes if the offer is clean. A good provider with a full Friday and a full Tuesday afternoon will say no, and that's fine. The right answer is not a 100% conversion rate. The right answer is finding the providers for whom this is genuinely useful.

2. The vetting IS the product. If a provider's vibe is off, if their references are vague, if they push back on the no-sexual-services policy — they don't get listed. There is no version of this where we accept questionable providers because we need supply. One bad provider can end the company. Your gut on this is the most important asset we have.

If you're unsure about a provider, say so.

"Let me think about it and get back to you in a few days" is a complete answer. There's no pressure to onboard fast. There's pressure to onboard right.

— Marcus
