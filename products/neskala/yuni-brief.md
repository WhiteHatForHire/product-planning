For Yuni

A two-page brief on the project, the role, and what would happen if you said yes.

From Marcus  ·  April 2026  ·  Working title: Neskala

Read this first.

Yuni,

You asked the Momoyo corporate team better questions on that first call than most experienced investors would. You got the territory data, the pricing breakdown, the royalty confirmation, the location criteria. That initiative matters and I noticed it.

We did serious diligence on the franchise. The work was not wasted — you now understand how franchises are structured, how to read an investment breakdown, how to think about unit economics. That education is yours.

But I want to be honest: the franchise is someone else's system, someone else's supply chain, someone else's rules. I have been in that situation before — investing in a physical operation from a distance — and the gravity always pulls you in as the shadow operator. I do not want that pattern again.

What follows is a different idea. Built around what you are actually good at: Bahasa, local relationships, the ability to walk into a room and make practitioners feel genuinely seen. Those are not supporting features of this business. They are the engine.

I am giving you genuine permission to say no. This only makes sense if the work feels alive to you. If recruiting massage therapists, pitching villas, and coordinating bookings sounds draining rather than exciting — tell me honestly. We find something else. No pressure either way.

— Marcus

What it is

A simple idea: most tourists in Bali don't know which massage therapist or healer they actually need. They Google, they find 40 results, none of them mean anything, and they end up at a hotel spa or a Klook booking they don't really trust.

We give them something better. They scan a QR code at their villa or guesthouse. They tell us how they're feeling — sore, jet-lagged, anxious, post-yoga, whatever. Our system matches them with someone good. We explain why this person fits them. You confirm the booking by WhatsApp. The therapist shows up. The customer pays cash. Everyone wins.

Behind the scenes it's a working app. In front of you it's relationships, WhatsApp messages, and a small dashboard.

What you'd actually do

Find the providers. Massage therapists, healers, bodyworkers in Ubud who you trust. People you already know or can get to through one introduction. Goal: 10 good ones in 30 days. Probably 30–40 conversations to get there.

Vet them. 30-minute interview, photo, reference check. The vetting is the product. If you wouldn't send your sister to them, don't list them.

Sign 5 villa partners. Front desks at villas and guesthouses in Ubud. They put a small QR card at reception. They get paid Rp 25–50k per completed booking from their guests.

Coordinate the bookings. When a guest scans and submits a request, you check the provider's availability via WhatsApp, confirm with the customer, send the brief in Bahasa to the provider so they know what to expect.

Capture what you learn. Voice memo on your phone after every provider meeting. Quick notes after every session. The system turns those into structured records — you don't have to type anything formal.

Tell me what's true. If providers are unreliable, if customers are weird, if villa managers don't understand it, if anything feels off — tell me right away. Your read of the local situation is the whole point.

What you get paid

Phase 0 — Trial (first 30 days, part-time)

Project stipend: Rp 1.5–2m for the month

Per provider you onboard: Rp 75–100k bonus

Per booking you coordinate: Rp 15–20k

Keep your Marco job. Stay part-time.

No equity yet. No exceptions on either side.

Phase 1 — Part-Time Launch Lead (after 10+ bookings)

Rp 2.5–3.5m/month base, still part-time

Same per-provider and per-booking bonuses continue

Phase 2 — Full-Time (after 50+ bookings/month)

Rp 4–4.5m/month base — replaces Marco income

Phantom equity or profit-share discussion opens

Phase 3 — Head of Bali (after 150+/month + expansion signal)

Rp 5–7m/month + performance bonus + formal vesting equity (2–5%, 2-year vest)

If we stop

If I stop the experiment for any reason after you've started fieldwork:

Rp 500,000 per provider you onboarded, plus Rp 1,000,000 per villa or guesthouse partner you signed

Capped at Rp 8,000,000 total

Plus all unpaid stipend and bonuses to that point

The provider relationships you built are yours. Take them anywhere — including a future business of your own.

This is in writing from day one. The relationship matters more than the project.

The 30-day test

Four weeks. Two questions:

Does it work? Did we get 10 providers, 3+ partners actively referring, and 10–15 paid sessions?

Does the work feel alive? Honest self-check. If recruiting providers and coordinating bookings feels energising, we keep going. If it feels draining, we stop and do something else. No bad outcome here.

If both are yes, we move to Phase 1. If either is no, we have a clean stopping point and you've learned a lot — about provider networks, about marketplace mechanics, about Bali's wellness scene from the inside. None of that is wasted.

How you say no

Just tell me. No script needed. Reasons that matter:

This sounds draining, not exciting

I don't want to recruit strangers in this way

The pace doesn't fit my life right now

I want to focus on Marco / something else

It feels too startup-y / too risky / too whatever

Or any other reason. There is no version of this where I'm disappointed. The franchise diligence already taught both of us something useful, and saying no to one project doesn't close any other doors.
