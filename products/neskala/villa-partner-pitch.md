Villa Partner Pitch

What Yuni reads before her first villa & guesthouse conversations.

Internal use  ·  English only  ·  Yuni adapts to each conversation

Read this first.

Villa partners aren't a passive referrer. They're a customer of the system. Their job is to feel like they got a service, not like they did us a favor.

The single most important framing:

"This makes your guests' stay better, with zero work for you and a small commission per booking."

Not: "please put our QR card at reception."

Not: "we're a startup, will you support us."

Not: "we'll bring you tons of bookings."

They have one currency that matters: guest experience. Anything that risks it is a no. Anything that improves it is a yes. Make the conversation about that.

Who to target — and who not to

Target: small-to-mid-tier villas and guesthouses where guest experience is personal.

4–20 room villas and guesthouses in Ubud, Penestanan, Sayan, Sanur

Owner-managed or with a hands-on manager who has authority to say yes

Wellness-adjacent positioning — yoga retreats, spiritual retreats, mid-tier wellness resorts

Places where guests already ask reception "can you recommend a massage?"

Properties without an in-house spa or with a spa they don't push

Don't target:

Big-brand hotels — too many decision-makers, too many compliance reviews, no quick yes

Properties with their own working spa or contracted therapists — we threaten their existing arrangement

Front desks where the manager is absent or staff can't make this call

Anywhere the vibe feels transactional or chain-like — they won't sell our story to a guest

The core pitch — what to actually say

After greetings, after small talk, after positioning yourself as a fellow Bali wellness operator — the substance is this:

"You probably have guests who ask the front desk if you can recommend a massage. We're a small team building something for exactly those guests. They scan a QR card at your reception, tell our system how their body and mind are feeling, and we match them with one of the local therapists or healers we've personally vetted. We confirm everything by WhatsApp before anything is final, and the therapist comes to your guest's room or villa.

"What it means for you: you can keep recommending the way you already do, but you can also point guests to the QR card when you're busy or unsure who to suggest. Your guests get a better experience than booking a random Klook or hotel-spa session. You don't have to vet anyone, manage anyone, or take responsibility for anyone — we do all of that.

"How it works for you: small QR card at reception, that's it. We pay you a commission for every completed booking from your property — Rp 25,000 to 50,000 per session, paid monthly via bank transfer. No fee, no contract you can't exit, no exclusivity. If it doesn't work out, take the card down and we walk away. Two weeks, no risk on your side."

The objections — and the answers

"I already have a therapist I recommend."

Answer: "Of course — and please keep recommending them. We're not asking for exclusivity. The QR card is for the times when your therapist isn't available, when a guest wants something specific you don't usually offer, or when you don't want to be the one making the recommendation. Think of it as a backup that works as well as your main one."

"What if a guest has a bad experience? It reflects on us."

Answer: "That's why we vet every provider personally. I interview each of them, check references, monitor their first three sessions. We carry the responsibility, not you. If anything goes wrong with a guest, you tell me directly and I handle it. The card has a clear note that this is an independent service, separate from your property."

"How much commission do we get?"

Answer: "Rp 25,000 per standard 60-minute booking, Rp 50,000 per couples or 90-minute booking. Premium healer sessions are higher — typically Rp 75,000 to 150,000. Paid monthly via bank transfer, with a clear list of every booking from your property so you can verify. We're testing the rate during the first 30 days, so if it feels low we can revisit."

"Can you guarantee bookings?"

Answer: "No, and I won't pretend to. Your guests scan the card if they're interested. Some will, some won't. What I can tell you is that the guests who do book come back happier than they would from a random booking — and a happier guest is a better review for you. Ask me again in two weeks and I'll tell you exactly how many scans and bookings came from your property."

"How is this different from Klook or other massage apps?"

Answer: "Klook lists everything — the customer has to figure out what they want. We ask the customer how they're feeling and match them to the right person. Different problem, different solution. The therapists we work with mostly aren't on Klook because they don't want to be on a price-shopping marketplace."

"What about the legal side — taxes, business permits?"

Answer: "You're not selling anything or taking payment, so it doesn't change your tax situation. Our company handles the booking and pays providers directly. The commission to you is a referral fee for each completed booking — we'll send you a simple monthly statement that you can use however you need. If you want it routed differently, tell me what works for you."

What you actually need from them

If they say yes (or seem interested):

Get the right contact person — owner, manager, or whoever has authority to put a card at reception. Phone + WhatsApp + email.

Understand their guest profile: how many rooms, average stay length, where guests come from, how often guests ask about wellness.

Understand their existing setup: do they have an in-house spa, a regular therapist they recommend, a relationship that could conflict with us.

Get a soft commitment: "yes, I'll put the card at reception for two weeks and we'll see."

Drop off the QR card on the same visit if you have one with you. Show them exactly where it should sit on the desk.

Set the expectation: "I'll check in with you in two weeks to see how it's going." Then actually do it.

Record a 60-second voice memo right after the meeting. Property name, contact, vibe, anything specific they said.

If they say no — capture why.

Already have a therapist / worried about guest experience / commission too low / can't decide alone / no reception desk / not enough wellness guests / wants exclusive deal. Each "no" tells us something about the offer or the targeting. Five no's in the same category and we revise.

Commission tiers (for your reference)

What the partner gets per completed booking from their property:

Standard 60-min massage: Rp 25,000

90-minute or couples massage: Rp 50,000

Premium healer or specialist (Rp 1.5M+ session): Rp 75,000 – 150,000

Repeat bookings from same guest: same rate, no decay

Paid monthly.

Bank transfer. With a line-item list of every booking, so they can verify against their own front-desk log.

What we are NOT offering:

No upfront fee. No commitment. No exclusivity. No revenue share on guest spending outside our platform.

No promises on volume — they'll see what they see.

No "join our marketing program" framing — this is a referral relationship, not a partnership announcement.

Two things to remember

1. The card matters less than the relationship. Anyone can put a QR code on a desk. The reason this works is that the front-desk person actively points guests to it when asked "can you recommend a massage?" — and they only do that if they trust you and you've made it easy. The first conversation is about earning that trust. The card is the artifact, not the pitch.

2. Two weeks, no risk, walks-away-clean. Every villa partner conversation should end with a low-stakes ask: try it for two weeks. If they don't see scans, take the card down and we go quietly. That framing is what gets the yes. Anyone who wants more commitment up front is too cautious to be a good first partner anyway.

If a partner wants to think about it, they get one follow-up.

Send a short WhatsApp 5–7 days later: "Hey, just checking in — would Friday work to drop off the card and have a quick chat?" If still unsure, leave it. Don't chase. The next yes is a faster route than convincing this no.
