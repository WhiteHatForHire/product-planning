Tier 1 — Build these first (this week)

1. The 2-Page Yuni Brief

Highest priority. You can’t responsibly hand v6.3 to Yuni — it’s 80+ pages and most of it is operator-grade detail she doesn’t need. The Brief opens with the Letter (verbatim from v6.3), then: what she does, what she’s paid (the staged comp + kill-fee), what the 30-day test looks like, how she says no, what happens if it stops. Two pages. That’s it.

This blocks the conversation with her. Until she has this, you can’t have the conversation.

2. The Provider Pitch (1 page)

What Yuni reads and internalizes before walking into Amaji’s home or texting Alexa. Names them and the Russian Karsai healer explicitly so the texture is concrete. Opens with the provider’s POV (“you’re already booked through word-of-mouth, why join us?”), addresses the foreign-card payment moat as the genuine differentiator, names the no-exclusivity policy, ends with the ask. One page. Bahasa version too if you want.

This blocks the first 10 provider conversations.

3. The Build Brief (1 page)

Your Serious Build Checklist’s Section 8 explicitly calls for this. The audit doc I made flagged it as Gap #5. It’s the artifact you pin at the top of every Replit session: project name, mode (Pilot), credit cap (you still need to set this), timebox (1 week), stop condition, user, problem, smallest valuable loop, no-go scope, AI role, stack, smoke test, launch path, ops owner. Single page. You read it before you open Cursor.

This blocks the Replit sprint. You cannot build well without it.

Tier 2 — Build these soon (next two weeks)

4. The Concept One-Pager

For sharing with friends, advisors, curious operators who ask “what are you working on.” One page, visual, scannable. The pitch, the wedge, Sarah’s Wednesday in 80 words, the trust mechanism, the moat, the ask (right now: “let me know if you have therapist or villa contacts in Ubud”). This is the highest-leverage shareable artifact for casual conversations during the 30-day sprint.

5. The Villa Partner Pitch (1 page)

Standalone version of the script you have in Part 13. What Yuni hands to a villa manager in person, or sends as a follow-up after a quick conversation. Addresses the “we already have a guy” objection, shows the QR card mechanic, names the three commission tiers you’re testing (Rp 25k / Rp 50k / 10%), ends with “want to try it for two weeks?”

7. Technical Spec / API Documentation

After V1, when there’s something to document. Right now the v6.3 build spec section is the technical doc.

8. Press / Launch Copy

After the first 20 sessions, when you have Sarah-style customer quotes to anchor it.
