# Final Business Card Direction Doc

> Imported from the source archive and converted to Markdown for searchability. Treat the source path as provenance, not as the current canonical location.
Final Business Card Direction Doc

Marcus Vale / Symposium Studios

NYC Tech Week Print Direction

Executive decision

The card should be a two-sided identity system:

Front: Marcus Vale, personal calling card.
Back: Symposium Studios, studio/action side with QR.

The design direction is already right: warm ivory front, near-black back, restrained typography, muted gold rule, sparse layout. Keep that. The final pass should make the card more commercially legible, less over-navigated, and properly print-ready.

Final content

Front side

Use only:

Marcus Vale
Founder / Technical Director
Symposium Studios

No URLs on the front.
No QR code on the front.
No phone number.
No social handles.

Rationale: the front should feel like a handshake, not a navigation menu. It should create recognition and authority. The back handles follow-up.

Back side

Use:

Symposium Studios

Senior-led development for prototypes, product systems, and LLM-integrated applications.

Scan for selected work, writing, builds, and contact.

symposiumstudios.ai/marcus

Include the real QR code for:

https://symposiumstudios.ai/marcus

The QR URL must be live before printing.

Title decision

Use:

Founder / Technical Director

Do not use:

Systems Architect

Reason: in a NYC tech room, “Systems Architect” can read as a narrow enterprise or infrastructure role. “Founder / Technical Director” better communicates seniority, judgment, direction, product leadership, and build capability without sounding inflated.

Acceptable alternate if the card needs to be more explicitly hireable:

Founder / Fractional CTO

But the preferred version is:

Founder / Technical Director

Production dimensions

Design for VistaPrint standard horizontal business cards.

VistaPrint’s US standard business card trim size is 3.5 × 2 inches. Their dimension guide lists standard full bleed at 3.61 × 2.11 inches / 1083 × 633 px, trim at 3.50 × 2.00 inches / 1050 × 600 px, and safe area at 3.36 × 1.86 inches / 1008 × 558 px. (VistaPrint)

Use the VistaPrint template for the exact product selected, but the design target should be:

Bleed canvas: 1083 × 633 px
Trim: 1050 × 600 px
Safe area: 1008 × 558 px
Resolution: 300 DPI
Orientation: horizontal

Keep all text, QR, rules, and important elements inside the safe area.

Typography direction

Front typography

Use a refined serif for:

Marcus Vale

Good references:

Cormorant Garamond

EB Garamond

Canela-like editorial serif

Use a clean sans serif for:

Founder / Technical Director
Symposium Studios

Good references:

Inter

Neue Haas Grotesk

Helvetica Now

SF Pro

Avoid excessive letter spacing. The earlier mockups had too much tracking in the small text. Tighten it for real card-size readability.

Back typography

Recommended: use a clean sans serif for most or all back text.

The back can still use a serif for “Symposium Studios” if it visually matches the front, but the safer direction is slightly more architectural and practical than the front.

Avoid:

decorative type

over-wide tracking

tiny thin text

AI-generated-looking letterforms

Front layout

Required layout behavior

The front should be sparse and confident.

Recommended hierarchy:

Marcus Vale

Founder / Technical Director

Symposium Studios

Composition

Title below

Optional thin muted-gold rule beneath the identity block

No bottom URL row

Front note

Earlier front mockup was strong, but too much small text and navigation. Final version should be simpler.

The front should make someone think:

This person has taste. This person is serious. I should remember this name.

Back layout

Required layout behavior

The back should convert attention into action.

Recommended structure:

Left side:

Symposium Studios

short muted-gold or stone accent rule

descriptor

scan CTA

Right side:

real QR code on white block

symposiumstudios.ai/marcus beneath or near QR

QR rules

Use the real QR asset. Do not use an AI-generated QR.

QR should be:

black on white

high contrast

not stylized

not blended into the black background

placed with its quiet zone intact

visually important but not dominant

Reduce QR size versus the current mockup by about 15 to 25 percent.

Target printed size:

roughly 0.8 to 1 inch square

The QR is a utility element, not the hero. The studio positioning should still have presence.

QR destination requirement

Before printing, confirm:

https://symposiumstudios.ai/marcus

is live, mobile-friendly, and not a 404.

Minimum QR page content:

Marcus Vale
Founder / Technical Director, Symposium Studios

Buttons in this order:

Book a fit call

View Symposium Studios

Selected builds / Anchor

Read “Solo, But Not Alone”

Marcus Vale essays

Email

LinkedIn / GitHub

This page should feel like a serious digital calling card, not a generic Linktree clone.

What to preserve from the mockups

Preserve:

cream front / black back system

muted gold rule

large serif Marcus Vale name

clear two-sided architecture

premium restraint

QR on the back

studio descriptor copy

overall sparse feel

What to change from the mockups

Change:

remove front URLs

replace “Founder / Systems Architect” with “Founder / Technical Director”

reduce QR size on the back

use real QR artwork

tighten letter spacing

make all typography real and print-safe

rebuild as a controlled design file, not as raw AI image output

Print-readiness warning

Do not upload the AI mockup directly as the final print file.

The final file should be rebuilt in Canva, Figma, Illustrator, or equivalent using:

real text layers

real fonts

exact dimensions

real QR code SVG/PNG

correct bleed and safe margins

export as PDF Print or 300 DPI PNG

Final verdict

This should be a quiet, high-taste, serious card.

The front creates intrigue and authority.
The back explains the studio and gives a clear next action.
The QR page carries the complexity.

Do not over-explain on the card. Let the card open the door. Let the QR page do the routing.
